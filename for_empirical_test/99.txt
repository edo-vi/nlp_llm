
Fragmenta de viribus is a homeopathy
The book was written by Samuel Hahnemann and published in Latin in two volumes  The full title is  Fragmenta de viribus medicamentorum: positivis sive in sano corpore humano observatis (Fragmentary Observations relative to the Positive Powers of Medicines on the healthy Human Body)

The first volume of Fragmenta de viribus lists the detailed Symptom
Fragmenta de viribus lists the health effects of 27 drugs in common use as recorded in the medical literature along with Hahnemanns own observations from taking the drugs himself: "The first collection of the effects of medicines  according to his own observations and those of others was as is well known published in the work Fragmenta de viribus medicamentorum 1805" These were "medicinal substances whose pure pathogenetic action he had ascertained by experiments on himself his family and a few friends" 

Fragmenta de viribus makes no mention of homeopathy or any theories of cure and makes no claims about their uses However it is widely regarded as the forerunner of Hahnemanns Materia Medica Pura part one of which was published in 1811: "the Materia Medica Pura had had its precursor in the Fragmenta" "The work begun in the "Fragmenta" was continued in the "Materia Medica Pura" the first volume of which appeared in 1811 followed by five more volumes up to 1821"
