

A soliloquy ( from Latin solo "to oneself" + loquor "I talk" plural soliloquies) is a monologue addressed to oneself thoughts spoken out loud without addressing another

Soliloquies are used as a device in drama to let a character make their thoughts known to the audience address it directly or take it into their confidence wholly or in part English Renaissance theatre
