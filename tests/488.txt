
Madhava (or Madhavakara) was a 7thcentury or early 8thcentury Indian Ayurveda practitioner who wrote the Rugvinischaya also known as the Madhava Nidana which soon assumed a position of authority In the 79 chapters of this book he lists diseases along with their causes symptoms and complications He also included a chapter on smallpox (masūrikā)
