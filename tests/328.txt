Live virus reference strain (LVRS) refers to a common strain of a virus that is selected for the manufacture of a Vaccine
 Annual Influenza vaccine development 
Each year with the assistance of the World Health Organization the Centers for Disease Control in Atlanta Georgia select strains of Influenza virus that are most likely to provide effective immunization to a broad spectrum of individuals

Vaccine viruses are chosen to maximize the likelihood that the vaccine will protect against the viruses most likely to spread and cause illness among people during the upcoming flu season WHO recommends specific vaccine viruses for influenza vaccine production but then individual countries make their own decisions for licensing of vaccines in their country In the United States the Food and Drug Administration determines what viruses will be used in USlicensed vaccines
