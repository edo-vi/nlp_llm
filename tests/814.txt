Porcine zona pellucida (PZP) is the zona pellucida extracted from the ovaries of pigs which is used as a source of antigens for immunocontraception

The zona pellucida is a thick membrane that surrounds the unfertilised eggs of mammals In order for an egg to be fertilised sperm must first bind to and then penetrate the zona pellucida When porcine (pig) zona pellucida is injected into other mammals antibodies are produced which attach to that animals zona pellucida preventing the sperm from attaching to the egg therefore preventing fertilisation

Porcine zona pellucida has been used in wildlife contraceptive
